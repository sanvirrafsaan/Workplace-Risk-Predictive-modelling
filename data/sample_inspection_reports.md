# Sample Inspection Reports

## Sample Report 1

On December 6, 2024, John Smith was working at Him Torhon’s, located at 123 Main street, in the City of Toronto. The trained forklift operator was operating a Raymond Reach lifting device and was trying to load a filing cabinet into a van. The vehicle was unable to drive into the shipping bay (drive-in dock), due to the ladder rack on the top of the van. So the van was positioned as close as it could, on the drive up dock ramp – perpendicular to the drive-in door, outside, in front of the bay door. The van was positioned this way so that Mr. Smith could use the lifting device to place the filing cabinet inside the side door of the van.

When Mr. Smith was manoeuvring the lifting device, outside of the building, on the drive up dock ramp, his machine caught the slope of the ramp and the lifting device rolled to side, toward the curb of the ramp. During this time, the momentum caused Mr. Smith right leg to swing out of the Raymond Reach compartment and his left foot became pinned between the curb and the lifting device.

Mr. Smith called for help and other workers came to help release Mr. Smith. The other workers were able to use another Raymond to free Mr. Smith.

Emergency services were called, and Mr. Smith was taken to Toronto General Hospital.

Upon investigation of the incident, there is no follow up action required by the Ministry of Labour, Immigration, Training and Skills Development.

## Sample Report 2

| Facility Name | Fake Ontario Metal Fabric Co. |
| --- | --- |
| Location / Sector | Sudbury/Timmins Region, Sector: Industrial/Metal Foundry |
| Date / Time | March 20, 2025 / 10:15 AM EST |

On March 17, 2025, at approximately 2:30 PM, a catastrophic mechanical failure occurred in the staging area of Bay 4 (Tube & Extrusion Finishing). A temporary heavy-duty metal holding rack, utilized to secure bundles of industrial structural steel tubes, suffered a structural failure. A loose metal holder/retaining pin sheared off under load.

Consequently, approximately twelve (12) heavy structural steel tubes—each weighing roughly 450 kg became unrestrained and rolled down the staging incline. Two production workers standing in the immediate transit path were struck and run over by the moving tubes, sustaining critical, life-threatening crush injuries. Emergency services were dispatched immediately, and the Ministry of Labour was notified as per legislative mandates.

Interviews with floor staff and a review of the morning shift logs reveal a gross absence of mandatory competent supervision at the time of the failure:

- The designated Bay 4 Area Supervisor was reassigned to a morning production meeting in an administrative building. No competent alternate or backup supervisor was appointed to overlook Bay 4 during this period.
- Prior to leaving the floor, the supervisor instructed worker A (21 years old, 3 weeks at the job) and worker B (24 years old, 11 months at the job) to manually clear floor debris directly downstream of the uncertified staging rack. Because they were new, they complied with a high-risk directive without understanding the vulnerability of their physical position.
An immediate post-incident audit of the workers' gear was conducted at the scene. Both workers were in 100% compliance with standard plant PPE requirements. However, standard PPE offers zero structural protection against a catastrophic crush force involving approximately 5,400 kg of rolling steel. The failure was entirely systemic and mechanical, not a failure of personal compliance.

Orders were issued under OHSA section 25(2)(a) due to failure to provide information, instruction, and supervision to a worker to protect their health or safety (specifically regarding new, young workers) and Reg 851, Section 7 for failure to conduct an engineering pre-start health and safety review on modified metal holders.

## Sample Report 3

On May 26, 2026, Bruce Lee (injured worker), employed by Toronto District School Board as a School Assistant at NoName Public School, located at 100 Main Street, ON, A1B C3D, suffered a fracture to Right Shoulder due to fall to the ground on same level in the yard area of the school.

The incident was captured on surveillance camera of the school.

The investigation concluded that the injured worker was a part-time non-union employee who is tasked with supervising primary and junior grade students during their lunch break. It was reported that the break consists of 20 mins inside the school in the lunch area and 40 mins outside in the yard area and the injured worker supervises primary and junior grade students both inside the school and in the yard during break. Discussion with workplace parties indicated that the school has, primary, junior and intermediate grade students. It was indicated that intermediate students have a designated play area during the break which is few meters apart from the primary to junior students.

On the day of the incident at around 12.30pm, when injured worker was supervising kids in the yard, a group of intermediate students running across the yard crossed their designated play area and upon approaching the injured worker, a student pulled right arm of the injured worker trying to run past the worker causing the injured worker to fall to the ground. Discussion with workplace parties indicated that the students involved have been spoken to and further it was indicated that as a long-term measure, it was the employer is pursuing the option of having more supervisors in the play area during lunch break.

It was reported that the primary-junior students are designated to play on the west side of the play area and intermediate students are designated to play on the east side. At the time of this visit, surveillance footage was played for this inspector which indicated that a group of students were observed running from east to west in the play area towards the injured worker and grabbing the worker and causing the fall.

Injured worker was spoke to over phone by this inspector. Discussion indicated that the worker was wearing a high visibility vest and footwear as required by the employer.

No uneven ground or any raised grounds were observed at the incident area. The weather was clear and sunny on the day and time of the incident, the ground was clear and dry.

No further MLITSD involvement is required at this time.
